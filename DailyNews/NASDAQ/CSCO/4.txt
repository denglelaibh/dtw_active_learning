Cisco Systems, Inc. (NASDAQ:CSCO) and Juniper Networks, Inc. (NASDAQ:JNPR) are both Technology companies that recently hit new highs. Many investors are wondering what to do with these names trading at such extreme levels. To determine if one is a better investment than the other, we will compare the two across growth, profitability, risk, return, dividends, and valuation measures.Cisco Systems, Inc. (NASDAQ:CSCO) operates in the Networking & Communication Devices segment of the Technology sector. The company has grown sales at a 0.80% annual rate over the past five years, putting it in the low growth category. CSCO has a net profit margin of 20.30% and is more profitable than the average company in the Networking & Communication Devices industry. In terms of efficiency, CSCO has an asset turnover ratio of 0.39. This figure represents the amount of revenue a company generates per dollar of assets. CSCO’s financial leverage ratio is 0.99, which indicates that the company’s asset base is primarily funded by equity capital. Company’s return on equity, which is really just the product of the company’s profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage ratios, is 14.80%, which is better than the Networking & Communication Devices industry average ROE.Cisco Systems, Inc. (CSCO) pays out an annual dividend of 1.16 per share. At the current valuation, this equates to a dividend yield of 3.07%. The company has a payout ratio of 56.70%. CSCO’s current dividend therefore should be sustainable. Stock’s free cash flow yield, which represents the amount of cash available to investors before dividends, expressed as a percentage of the stock price, is 0.8. All else equal, companies with higher FCF yields are viewed as cheaper. Company trades at a P/E ratio of 19.59, and is more expensive than the average stock in the Networking & Communication Devices industry. The average investment recommendation for CSCO, taken from a group of Wall Street Analysts, is 2.00, or a buy.Over the past three months, Cisco Systems, Inc. insiders have been net sellers, acquiring a net of 201,225 shares. This implies that insiders have been feeling relatively bullish about the outlook for CSCO. Insider activity and sentiment signals are important to monitor because they can shed light on how “risky” a stock is perceived to be at it’s current valuation. Knowing this, it makes sense to look at beta, a measure of market risk. CSCO has a beta of 1.17 and therefore an above average level of market volatility.
