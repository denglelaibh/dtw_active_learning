Of course, these surface-level price movements don’t tell us much about the direction that MDLZ may be headed in the future. If we want to get a sense of MDLZ’s future performance, we have to look at the speed and size of these price movements. Using what are known as momentum indicators, we can use the stock’s price momentum to get a sense of whether the stock is in the midst of, or nearing the end of, a current trend. Two such indicators are the RSI (Relative Strength Index) and the Stochastic %k Oscillator, which fluctuate on a scale of 0 to 100. A reading above 70 indicates that a stock is overvalued, and a reading below 30 implies that it is undervalued. The 20-day RSI for MDLZ is 54.82%, which suggests that the stock is not particularly expensive or cheap, and not predisposed to a reactive price movement based on this measure.
