This figure represents the amount of revenue a company generates per dollar of assets. MSFT’s financial leverage ratio is 1.71, which indicates that the company’s asset base is primarily funded by equity capital. Company’s return on equity, which is really just the product of the company’s profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage ratios, is 29.20%, which is better than the Business Software & Services industry average ROE.Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) pays out an annual dividend of 1.68 per share. At the current valuation, this equates to a dividend yield of 2.00%. The company has a payout ratio of 52.20%. MSFT’s current dividend therefore should be sustainable. Stock’s free cash flow yield, which represents the amount of cash available to investors before dividends, expressed as a percentage of the stock price, is 1.12. All else equal, companies with higher FCF yields are viewed as cheaper. Company trades at a P/E ratio of 28.37, and is less expensive than the average stock in the Business Software & Services industry. The average investment recommendation for MSFT, taken from a group of Wall Street Analysts, is 1.80, or a buy.Over the past three months, Microsoft Corporation insiders have been net sellers, acquiring a net of 16,275 shares. This implies that insiders have been feeling relatively bullish about the outlook for MSFT. Insider activity and sentiment signals are important to monitor because they can shed light on how “risky” a stock is perceived to be at it’s current valuation. Knowing this, it makes sense to look at beta, a measure of market risk. MSFT has a beta of 1.00 and therefore an average level of market volatility.
